Do I Really Understand DNS?

You might think you understand DNS, but when you find yourself taking random shots in the dark while debugging DNS issues, it's time to reassess. Let's break down some key DNS concepts to sharpen our understanding and improve our troubleshooting skills.

1. Do I understand what a resolver is?

  • It's a service on your machine that handles domain name resolution.
  • It requests IP addresses from nameservers or checks its cache.
  • Different OSes use different resolver daemons:
    • Linux: systemd-resolved, dnsmasq, BIND (named)
    • MacOS: mDNSResponder

Test your knowledge: Can you check which resolver is running on your Linux machine?

ps aux | grep -e systemd-resolved -e dnsmasq -e named

2. Can I explain what nameservers do?

  • They contain DNS records and return mappings of domain names to IP addresses.
  • Example record: google.com. 59 IN A 142.250.70.46
  • Do you know what each part of this record means?

3. Am I familiar with resolver configuration?

  • The /etc/resolv.conf file contains directives for the resolver service.
  • Can you explain the difference between 'domain' and 'search' directives?
  • Do you know what the 'nameserver' directive does?

4. Do I understand the nameserver hierarchy?

  • Root Nameservers: The highest level in the DNS hierarchy.
  • Top-Level Domain (TLD) Nameservers: Handle queries for specific TLDs (.com, .org, etc.)
  • Specific Domain Nameservers: Provide the final answer in DNS resolution

If you found yourself unsure about any of these points, it might be time to dive deeper into DNS concepts. Understanding these fundamentals will greatly improve your ability to troubleshoot DNS-related issues effectively.
